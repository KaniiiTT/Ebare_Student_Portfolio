    What is Cyberbullying?

    Cyberbullying is using technology to harm, threaten, or embarrass someone. It can happen through messages, social media posts, or even online games. Knowing the signs helps protect yourself and others.

    1. Mean or Hurtful Messages

    Sending offensive or threatening messages online or through social media platforms is a common form of cyberbullying.

    2. Fake Profiles or Impersonation

    Some bullies create fake accounts to post rumors or pretend to be someone else to embarrass others.

    3. Public Shaming

    Posting private photos or messages publicly to humiliate someone can deeply harm mental health.

    4. Exclusion from Groups

    Leaving someone out of group chats or online games on purpose can also be a form of bullying.
